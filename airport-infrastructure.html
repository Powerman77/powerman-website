Pre-Feasibility Study

A Pre-Feasibility Study for a greenfield airport is an initial strategic assessment that evaluates overall project viability before significant resources are committed. It examines critical factors including technical infrastructure requirements, estimated financial costs, market demand, regulatory compliance, and environmental impacts — providing an early indication of whether the project should advance to detailed planning and investment stages.

Our team brings decades of experience conducting comprehensive feasibility studies for airports and helipads. Our reports are built on rigorous research and cover:

  • Land requirements and site suitability
  • Runway orientation and dimensional specifications
  • Airside and terminal building requirements
  • Licensing requirements and regulatory alignment
  • Coordination with civil and defence authorities
  • ATS route infrastructure planning
  • Siting of Communication, Navigation & Surveillance (CNS) systems
  • Airport and airspace capacity evaluation
  • Financial viability assessment
A Pre-Feasibility Study helps stakeholders identify challenges early, align projects with broader objectives, and avoid unnecessary financial exposure — serving as a critical filter against high-risk or unfeasible ideas.
Who This Is For
  • Greenfield Airport Developers
  • State & Central Government Bodies
  • Private Infrastructure Investors
  • Industrial & SEZ Projects
  • UDAN Scheme Applicants
02
Safety

Obstacle Limitation Surface (OLS) Survey

OLS Surveys are essential for airport authorities, aviation regulators, and urban planners in maintaining flight safety and operational efficiency. These surveys support airspace design, infrastructure development, and obstacle clearance assessment.

The collected data is used to:

  • Monitor existing obstacles in and around the aerodrome
  • Plan mitigation strategies for identified hazards
  • Guide zoning and land-use decisions in airport vicinities
By identifying potential hazards proactively, OLS surveys contribute significantly to minimising risk and enabling smooth operations across both civil and military aviation.
Applications
  • Airport Authorities
  • Aviation Regulators
  • Urban & Regional Planners
  • Real Estate Developers near airports
  • Military Aviation Facilities

Operations

Flight Procedure Design

Flight Procedure Design develops safe and efficient routes for aircraft across all phases of flight — departures, en-route segments, arrivals, and approaches. Procedures are established using accurate navigation data, terrain assessments, and international standards set by ICAO.

  • Optimise available airspace utilisation
  • Reduce pilot workload
  • Guarantee obstacle clearance
  • Uphold stringent safety margins
  • Account for air traffic volumes, environmental factors, and airport infrastructure constraints

Carefully crafted flight procedures prevent airspace conflicts, lower fuel consumption, and reduce noise impacts on surrounding communities. They are especially critical in areas with challenging terrain or at high-traffic airports, where precision is non-negotiable.

All procedures must receive DGCA approval before implementation. Our team — comprising former Authorised Procedure Designers — provides comprehensive support for developing PBN-compliant procedures that enhance accessibility, safety, and operational efficiency.
Procedure Types
  • Departure Procedures (SID)
  • Arrival Procedures (STAR)
  • Instrument Approach Procedures
  • PBN / RNAV / RNP Procedures
  • Visual Flight Procedures
