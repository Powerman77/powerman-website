Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is a NOC for height clearance required for structures near airports? +
Structures near airports can pose a hazard to aircraft operations. The AAI requires a No Objection Certificate (NOC) for any building, mast, transmission line, or structure that may exceed permissible height limits in the vicinity of an airport — to ensure safe flight paths and operations.
What is NOCAS? +
NOCAS (NOC for Construction of Structures) is the online portal developed by AAI for submitting and tracking height clearance NOC applications. Applicants can access it via the AAI website at www.aai.aero or directly at nocas.aai.aero.
What is GSR 751(E)? +
GSR 751(E) is the Government of India gazette notification that prescribes the height restrictions for structures near airports and the mandatory requirement to obtain NOC from AAI before commencing construction of any structure that may exceed the permissible height limits.
Up to what distance from an airport is a NOC required? +
The distance varies depending on the type of airport and the nature of the structure. Typically, NOC requirements apply to structures within 20 km of an airport reference point, but the specific limits are defined by the airport's obstacle limitation surfaces and applicable regulations.
Who issues the height clearance NOC for civil airports? +
For civil airports managed by AAI, the respective Regional Headquarters of AAI issues the height clearance NOC. For defence airports, the relevant defence authority issues the clearance.
What is the validity of a NOC? +
NOCs issued by AAI are generally valid for 2 years from the date of issue. If construction is not completed within this period, the NOC must be revalidated before the expiry date.
What are WGS-84 coordinates and how do I get them? +
WGS-84 (World Geodetic System 1984) coordinates are the geographic coordinates required for NOCAS applications. They can be obtained through a licensed surveyor, GPS devices, or validated mapping tools. POWERMAN supports coordinate assessment and validation as part of our technical services.
Does AAI charge a fee for height clearance NOC? +
AAI does not charge a fee for the issuance of height clearance NOC under the current process. However, professional advisory fees for application preparation, documentation, and coordination services apply separately.
Can I start construction after submitting my NOC application? +
No. Construction cannot commence until the NOC is formally granted by AAI. Starting construction before receiving the NOC is a violation of aviation safeguarding regulations and may result in legal action and mandatory demolition.
What are common reasons for rejection of a NOC application? +
Common reasons include incorrect or unvalidated WGS-84 coordinates, inaccurate top elevation data, incomplete documentation, proposed height exceeding obstacle limitation surfaces, and missing or incorrect plot/site information. POWERMAN's pre-submission review helps identify and resolve these issues before filing.
What is the process for revalidation of a NOC? +
Revalidation must be applied for before the existing NOC expires. The applicant must submit a fresh application on NOCAS with updated documentation confirming no changes to the structure's coordinates, height, or usage. POWERMAN supports revalidation applications as part of our end-to-end service.
